Research Gap

What is Our Research Gap

This research identifies a gap in stress-relief apps for software engineers and suggests a specialized platform that combines emotional analysis with physical data from IoT devices for better stress assessment. Unlike self-report methods, it uses machine learning to analyze stress indicators and incorporates blockchain for secure health data management. This system could improve engineers' well-being and productivity by offering personalized stress management and ensuring data privacy and compliance with legal standards.

Literature Review

What is Our Research Gap

In the realm of mental health support, the inclination towards virtual assistants is driven by the challenges individuals face in seeking traditional therapy—whether due to the stigma associated with mental illness, financial constraints, or a preference for non-human interaction. Research often targets academia, medical professionals, or the general public, emphasizing the need for more inclusive solutions like virtual mental health assistants. Current studies highlight the role of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning in creating stress identification systems. Speech emotion recognition, analyzing speech tones for emotional cues, is explored for its potential in extracting vital information from human interaction. The integration of physiological and emotional states, including heart rate, blood oxygen levels, and facial expressions, is recognized as crucial for comprehensive approaches to emotional well-being. Addressing the need for task-based stress management, machine learning techniques, such as text classification and Support Vector Machines, are applied. The research emphasizes the importance of understanding the limitations of the Naive Bayes Classifier and the effectiveness of Support Vector Machines in text categorization. The proposed project advocates for a blockchain-based solution to ensure secure data storage and access control management, particularly in the context of employee well-being. Real-time data from AI chatbots and stress detection systems, coupled with K-Nearest Neighbors algorithm for stress-level categorization, forms the backbone of an automated system connecting employees with mental health professionals. While existing research extends to health monitoring and customized insurance policies, the distinct focus on IT specialists is identified as a gap. The project aims to fill this void by tailoring mental health support to the unique challenges faced by IT professionals, recognizing the heightened workplace stress within the IT sector and the need for specialized wellness initiatives.

Methodology

Our Methodology

The proposed research encompasses diverse methodologies to address stress identification and workload management for software engineers. One approach involves a voice-based chatbot application for stress identification, leveraging emotion analysis. Another employs IoT devices, Arduino C-coded boards, Laravel-based websites, and a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) algorithm for facial expression analysis to measure stress levels. Task allocation based on complexity and stress levels utilizes NLP techniques, training a model on historical work data. Lastly, a blockchain-based system is outlined for secure data storage and automated access control, directing individuals to mental health specialists based on stress indicators. Each method prioritizes data security, user-friendly interfaces, and compliance with legal standards, collectively contributing to a comprehensive solution for enhancing the well-being of software engineers.
